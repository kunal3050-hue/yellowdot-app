## Sprint 4 Scope ‚Äî what "Payment Lifecycle" concretely means

One genuinely new entity is required, plus extensions to two that already exist:

- **Payment, extended.** The existing `payments` collection (real, in production, written today by `invoiceService.recordPayment()` for the manual Invoice/PaymentDrawer flow) gains family-scoping and multi-target allocation, following the exact pattern M3.2 already proved for Invoices: a **new** `financePaymentService.js` writes into the **same** `payments` collection with additive fields (`familyId`, `allocations[]`, `source: "financeFoundation"` vs. the legacy flow's implicit `"manual"`), using its own distinct ID prefix and its own atomic counter ‚Äî never importing or modifying `invoiceService.js`, matching the now-established precedent. The legacy manual payment flow keeps working, untouched, exactly as invoice generation did not touch the legacy manual invoice flow in Sprint 3.
- **Payment Allocation, new (a thin structure, not a new top-level collection).** An array on the Payment document ‚Äî `{ studentLedgerId, invoiceId, amount }[]` ‚Äî recording exactly which Ledger(s)/Invoice(s) a Payment settled and by how much. This is the concrete mechanism behind "partial payments" and "multiple students in one family, one payment" (Chapter 1's Family Account design, validated in Sprint 3's Scenario 5 for billing ‚Äî Sprint 4 is the payment-side counterpart).
- **Receipt ‚Äî no new entity.** Per `02_DOMAIN_ARCHITECTURE.md`'s own note, the receipt-numbering mechanism is "already correct" ‚Äî `financePaymentService.js` will implement its **own** `FRCPT-YYYYMM-#####` counter (mirroring `financeInvoiceService.js`'s own `BINV-YYYYMM-#####`, not calling into `invoiceService.js`'s internal, unexported `genReceiptNumber()`), rather than the literal cross-import Chapter 2 originally suggested ‚Äî flagged here as a deliberate, small deviation from that earlier design note, consistent with the "never import `invoiceService.js`" precedent M3.2 already established and reaffirmed by the user's own Sprint 3 review ("extend existing architecture instead of replacing it" ‚Äî extending the *pattern*, not necessarily the *file*, where doing so would create cross-service coupling risk).

**The Payment flow itself, scoped narrowly for this sprint ‚Äî mapped directly to your eight bullets:**

1. **Payment recording** ‚Äî `financePaymentService.recordPayment()` creates the Payment document (amount, method, family, actor), immutable once created (matching the existing `firestore.rules` comment on `payments`: "generally immutable ‚Äî only admins may correct" ‚Äî Sprint 4 does not touch that rule).
2. **Payment allocation** ‚Äî a new `financePaymentAllocationService.js` (or a method on the payment service ‚Äî decided at build time) takes a recorded Payment and a target Family Account, and allocates it across that family's outstanding Student Ledgers, respecting the **already-existing** `familyAccountService`'s `paymentAllocationPreference` field (`"oldestDueFirst"` deterministic auto-allocation, or `"manual"` ‚Äî an explicit staff-specified per-ledger split). Each settled ledger gets one `type: "payment"` Ledger Entry, keyed `sourceType: "payment", sourceId: <paymentId>` ‚Äî idempotent by construction, per the Entry Criterion above.
3. **Partial payments** ‚Äî a natural consequence of allocation, not separate logic: if a Payment's amount is less than a ledger's outstanding balance, only that lesser amount is allocated to it (and, under `oldestDueFirst`, the remainder ‚Äî if any ‚Äî flows to the next-oldest outstanding ledger in the family).
4. **Overpayments** ‚Äî once every outstanding ledger in scope is fully settled, any amount still unallocated becomes Family Account **credit**, via the **already-existing, already-tested** `familyAccountService.adjustCreditBalance()` ‚Äî zero new code needed in that service, only a new caller. This directly closes the original Finance Module Audit's finding that the legacy flow lets an invoice's `balance` go negative with no credit concept at all.
5. **Credit balance handling** ‚Äî the reverse direction of #4: before a new charge requires a fresh payment, first consult the Family Account's `creditBalance` and auto-apply available credit as a `type: "creditApplied"` Ledger Entry (already a valid, correctly-signed entry type since Sprint 1, never yet posted by any caller). This is the natural extension point for both M3.4's invoice generation (a family with credit shouldn't need a fresh payment for a small new charge) and Sprint 4's own allocation flow.
6. **Refund and reversal workflow** ‚Äî two related but distinct actions, per `02_DOMAIN_ARCHITECTURE.md`'s own Refund entity design:
   - **Refund** (money paid back out): Requested ‚Üí Approved/Rejected ‚Üí Processed, gated by the **already-existing** `financeSettings.refundApprovalThreshold` field (defined in Sprint 1, never enforced by any caller until now ‚Äî same "field existed, no caller used it yet" story as `discountApprovalThreshold` was for M3.3 before this sprint). On approval, posts a `type: "refund"` Ledger Entry (already valid since Sprint 1) and reduces the Family Account credit balance if the refund is paid from credit, or is recorded as a straight cash-out otherwise.
   - **Reversal** (a Payment turns out to be invalid ‚Äî e.g. a bounced cheque): never edits or deletes the original Payment or its Ledger Entries (matching the immutability precedent already established for both) ‚Äî instead posts a new, offsetting Ledger Entry and marks the Payment's own status as `Reversed` (a new, additive status field on the Payment document, not a new collection).
7. **Full audit coverage** ‚Äî cross-cutting across every milestone below, not its own milestone: every Payment/Allocation/Refund/Reversal write calls `financeAuditService.logFinanceAudit()`, exactly as every Finance Platform write has since Sprint 1. Once M4.1‚ÄìM4.4 are built, a validation pass mirroring `08_SPRINT3_VALIDATION.md`'s realistic-scenario approach (real payment, partial payment, overpayment, refund, reversal, reconciling the audit trail against the resulting ledger state) is the natural way to prove this ‚Äî recommended as a follow-up validation step once the milestones below are implemented, not prescribed here as a numbered milestone of its own.

**Explicitly out of scope for Sprint 4** (unchanged from your instruction):
- **The recurring billing scheduler (M3.5)** ‚Äî still deferred. Nothing in Sprint 4 introduces automation of any kind; every action below remains staff- or gateway-webhook-confirmed, never a background job.
- **A real payment gateway integration** (Razorpay/PayU-style checkout, webhook reconciliation) ‚Äî the existing UPI-QR flow (`PaymentDrawer.jsx`) is a parent-facing payment *instruction*, not a Payment Foundation *concern*; Sprint 4 models the record of a payment however it was collected (cash, UPI, cheque, bank transfer, or eventually gateway), the same way the legacy `recordPayment()` already does today. A true gateway integration remains its own, larger, later piece of work.
- **GST-proper tax engine, Scholarship application** ‚Äî unchanged, already-documented gaps from earlier sprints, not this sprint's focus.
- **Deposit netting at Withdrawal/Settlement** ‚Äî `02_DOMAIN_ARCHITECTURE.md`'s Deposit entity is tied to the Withdrawal/Graduation Settlement engine (Chapter 1, M5), not the Payment lifecycle itself; out of scope here.

## Constraints (reaffirmed, unchanged from every prior sprint)

- No production behavior change until feature-flagged on ‚Äî `FINANCE_FOUNDATION_ENABLED` extends naturally to gate every new Payment endpoint; no new flag needed.
- Full backward compatibility ‚Äî the legacy manual payment flow (`invoiceService.recordPayment()`, `PaymentDrawer.jsx`) is not modified. A family's new, Finance-Foundation-recorded payments are additive; a legacy payment record remains valid and readable exactly as-is.
- Continued reuse ‚Äî `familyAccountService.adjustCreditBalance()`, `ledgerEntryService.createEntry()`'s existing `payment`/`refund`/`creditApplied` types and M3.1 idempotency, `financeSettings.refundApprovalThreshold`, and the `payments` collection itself are all extended, not replaced.
- No migration until explicitly approved ‚Äî no existing payment record is touched or backfilled by this sprint's work.
- Every write immutable-by-append, matching Ledger Entry's own precedent ‚Äî a Reversal is a new entry plus a status flip, never an edit to history.

## Proposed Milestones

**M4.1 ‚Äî Payment Domain Foundation (incorporates Recommendation 2 ‚Äî Payment State Machine)**
*Deliverables:* `services/financePaymentStateMachine.js` ‚Äî a new, pure, deterministic module (no Firestore, matching the `financeRulesEngine.js` precedent) defining the Payment lifecycle explicitly rather than as a free-form string: states `Pending` (reserved for a future gateway-confirmed flow ‚Äî no Sprint 4 caller reaches this state; every synchronous recordPayment() call creates directly in `Recorded`), `Recorded`, `Allocated`, `PartiallyAllocated`, `Refunded`, `PartiallyRefunded`, `Reversed`; an explicit transition map (`Recorded‚ÜíAllocated`, `Recorded‚ÜíPartiallyAllocated`, `Recorded‚ÜíReversed`, `PartiallyAllocated‚ÜíAllocated`, `PartiallyAllocated‚ÜíReversed`, `Allocated‚ÜíPartiallyRefunded`, `Allocated‚ÜíRefunded`, `Allocated‚ÜíReversed`, `PartiallyRefunded‚ÜíRefunded`; `Refunded`/`Reversed` are terminal ‚Äî no outgoing transitions); and `assertTransition(from, to)`, which every payment-mutating service (this one and every later milestone) calls before writing a new status, so an invalid transition is rejected in the service layer itself, never just assumed correct by the caller. `services/financePaymentService.js` ‚Äî `recordPayment()` writing into the existing `payments` collection with additive `familyId`/`source`/`allocations: []`/`status: "Recorded"` fields, its own `FPAY######` ID prefix (receipt numbering deferred to M4.3, per that milestone's own note). Frozen contract in `04_SERVICE_CONTRACTS.md`. *Dependencies:* none. *Risks:* low ‚Äî purely additive to a collection whose immutability rule already exists and isn't being changed; the main care item is proving the legacy `PaymentDrawer.jsx`/payment-history screens genuinely don't notice the schema extension, the same regression discipline M3.2 applied to `Invoice.jsx`. *Rollout:* behind `FINANCE_FOUNDATION_ENABLED`, no allocation logic yet ‚Äî a recorded-but-unallocated Payment is a valid intermediate state this milestone alone produces.

**M4.2 ‚Äî Payment Allocation Engine (incorporates Recommendation 1 ‚Äî Allocation Strategy Abstraction)**
*Deliverables:* `services/financeAllocationStrategies.js` ‚Äî a strategy registry (`{ oldestDueFirst, manual }`), each entry a pure function with the identical signature `strategy({ paymentAmount, outstandingLedgers, manualAllocations }) ‚Üí { allocations, leftoverAmount }`, so a future strategy (`highestAmountFirst`, `tuitionBeforeDaycare`, a school-specific custom policy) is a new registry entry, never a change to the allocation engine itself. `oldestDueFirst` orders a family's outstanding Student Ledgers by `createdAt` ascending ‚Äî documented honestly as ledger-granularity ordering ("the obligation open longest"), not true per-invoice due-date FIFO, since per-invoice aging isn't surfaced at the family level yet; `manual` takes a caller-supplied `{studentLedgerId, amount}[]` and validates it doesn't exceed the payment amount. `services/financePaymentAllocationService.js` ‚Äî the engine itself: resolves the family's `paymentAllocationPreference`, invokes the matching strategy, posts one idempotent `type: "payment"` Ledger Entry per settled ledger, routes any `leftoverAmount` to the existing `adjustCreditBalance()`, and transitions the Payment's status via `financePaymentStateMachine` (`Allocated` when `leftoverAmount` covers the full remainder, `PartiallyAllocated` when a manual allocation deliberately leaves some of the payment unallocated). *Dependencies:* M4.1. *Risks:* the highest-care milestone this sprint ‚Äî a mis-allocation is a real money-tracking bug, not a display issue; needs a scenario-based test matrix (exact-match, partial, overpayment, multi-ledger split, manual override) mirroring the rigor M3.3's proration matrix already got. *Rollout:* behind `FINANCE_FOUNDATION_ENABLED`, staff-initiated only. *Success criteria:* a staff member can record one payment against a family with multiple outstanding invoices across multiple children and see every ledger, the credit balance (if any), the Payment's own status, and the audit trail agree on where the money went.

**M4.3 ‚Äî Receipt Generation**
*Deliverables:* sequential `FRCPT-YYYYMM-#####` receipt record generated the instant a Payment is recorded (data/number only ‚Äî this milestone does not touch the existing, separately-flagged PDF-generation architecture gap from the original Finance Module Audit, which remains its own tech-debt item). *Dependencies:* M4.1. *Risks:* low ‚Äî a counter-generation pattern already proven twice (Invoice ID, Invoice Number). *Rollout:* behind `FINANCE_FOUNDATION_ENABLED`.

**M4.4 ‚Äî Credit Balance Consumption**
*Deliverables:* the *spending* side of the credit balance Sprint 4 starts *crediting* in M4.2 ‚Äî before a new charge requires a fresh payment, auto-apply available Family Account credit as a `type: "creditApplied"` Ledger Entry. Natural integration point for both a new Sprint 4 payment-allocation call and a future M3.4 invoice-generation call (a family with existing credit shouldn't need a fresh payment for a small new charge) ‚Äî this milestone builds the reusable function; wiring it into M3.4 itself is a small follow-up once this exists, not bundled into this milestone to keep it independently deployable. *Dependencies:* M4.1, M4.2 (needs the credit balance M4.2 already populates). *Risks:* moderate ‚Äî must never apply more credit than is available, and must never let two concurrent callers double-spend the same credit (reuses `adjustCreditBalance()`'s existing transactional guard, which already rejects a negative result). *Rollout:* behind `FINANCE_FOUNDATION_ENABLED`.

**M4.5 ‚Äî Refund & Reversal Workflow**
*Deliverables:* Refund: Requested ‚Üí Approved/Rejected ‚Üí Processed, gated by the existing (never-yet-enforced) `financeSettings.refundApprovalThreshold`; posts a `type: "refund"` Ledger Entry on approval, transitioning the Payment to `Refunded`/`PartiallyRefunded` via `financePaymentStateMachine.assertTransition()` (M4.1) ‚Äî an attempt to refund a Payment not currently in `Allocated` is rejected by the state machine itself, not left to the caller to remember. Reversal: transitions the Payment to `Reversed` (also state-machine-guarded) and posts an offsetting Ledger Entry ‚Äî never edits or deletes the original Payment or its entries. *Dependencies:* M4.1‚ÄìM4.4 (a refund/reversal acts on a Payment and may touch the credit balance both of those already built). *Risks:* the second-highest-care milestone this sprint, alongside Allocation ‚Äî an under-guarded refund approval is a real financial-control gap; the approver's authority must be validated server-side against their actual role/permission, never trusted from a client-supplied assertion (the same discipline `02_DOMAIN_ARCHITECTURE.md` already calls out by name, citing `userService.js`'s `ASSIGNABLE_ROLES` capping pattern as the model to follow). *Rollout:* behind `FINANCE_FOUNDATION_ENABLED`, staff-initiated only. *Success criteria:* a refund below threshold auto-processes; one at/above threshold blocks until an authorized approver acts; a reversal never mutates history, only appends to it.

**(Recommended, not a numbered milestone) Sprint 4 Validation** ‚Äî once M4.1‚ÄìM4.5 are implemented, a realistic-scenario validation pass mirroring `08_SPRINT3_VALIDATION.md` (real payment, partial payment, overpayment ‚Üí credit, credit consumption on a later charge, refund below/above threshold, reversal, full audit-trail reconciliation, cross-tenant isolation, RBAC) is the natural way to confirm the same "one coherent system" property Sprint 3 proved, extended one layer earlier in the chain.
